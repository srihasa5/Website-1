ABOUT

Albert Einstein (March 14, 1879 – April 18, 1955) was a German-born theoretical physicist who is widely regarded as one of the most influential scientists in history. He is best known for developing the theory of relativity, which transformed our understanding of space, time, and gravity. His iconic equation, E = mc², which expresses the equivalence of mass and energy, is among the most well-known formulas in science. Einstein’s special theory of relativity, published in 1905, revolutionized the concept of space and time, while his general theory of relativity, published in 1915, offered a new understanding of gravity, describing it as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. This groundbreaking work reshaped the field of physics and led to many subsequent discoveries.

BLOGS

Albert Einstein, one of the greatest minds in history, not only revolutionized science but also left behind profound life lessons. His words continue to inspire generations in various aspects, including personal growth, creativity, and leadership.

Think Differently: “Logic will get you from A to B. Imagination will take you everywhere.”
Knowledge vs. Imagination: “Imagination is more important than knowledge. Knowledge is limited. Imagination encircles the world.”
Simplicity: “If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it well enough.”
Embrace Change: “The measure of intelligence is the ability to change.”
Passion for Learning: “Intellectual growth should commence at birth and cease only at death.”
Be Ethical: “Relativity applies to physics, not ethics.”
Value of Questions: “The important thing is not to stop questioning. Curiosity has its own reason for existing.”
Live with Purpose: “Only a life lived for others is a life worthwhile.”
